Community

Within this small community there are numerous different nationalities and ethnic group that make this town so diverse. Most of the population comes from some sort of Asian decent, Chinese, South Asian, West Asian, Filipino, Japanese, and Korean. Those six groups make up roughly 10% of Redcliffâ€™s population and is continuing to grow each year. Other nationalities that populate this town are Latin American, Arab, and African American, which all together make up about 3% of the total population and the aboriginal community is marked as 5.8% of the total population.

What The Diversity Brings

Having such a wide range of diverse groups has allowed this town to grow and expand in unique ways. Bringing new experiences, restaurants/foods, traditions and has allowed this town to grow in ways many thoughts was not possible. Allowing the opportunity for everyone who visits to create memories that will stay with them for the rest of their lives.

Redcliff Minor Hockey Assosiation

redcliffminorhockey@hotmail.com

The Redcliff Minor Hockey Association is one of the many different local sports teams within this community. Ranging from ages as low as 4 years of age to 17 years of age, with 6 different age catagories, Initiation, Novice, Atom, Pee Wee, Bantam, and Midget. These teams travel all across Alberta to face-off against other Minor Hockey teams. These teams are ranked by tiers, tier 1 being the highest ranking and tier 5 being the lowest. These tiers are created to make and equal playing field for all teams and it does have fluidity regarding the tiers as teams can move up and/or down throughout the season.

Medicine Hat Pride Assosiation

info@yellow.place

The Medicine Hat Pride Association was founded in 2012 with a goal to promote and support the LGBTQ+ community and surrounding area.This organization stives to give the all people a better understanding of what the LGBTQ+ community is, they wish to see more acceptance, equality and inclusion throughout the city and surrounding area. They also provide support for both mental and physical health for those who identify within the LGBTQ+ and hope to make the health care system in Medicine Hat more inculisive and open. Prairie Rose School Division

communications@prrd8.ca

Prairie Rose School Division is made up of 18 different public schools and 18 Hutterite Colony schools/ Coulee Collegiate, supporting 3,300 students roughly in total between these 36 schools/ outreach programs. This district stretches across Redcliff, Oyen, Bow Island, Cypress County, and Foremost. Each sub-division ranges from grades K-12, with only 4 high schools, Eagle Butte High School in Dunmore, Alberta being the only feeder school within the division.
